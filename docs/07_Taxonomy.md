# Career Copilot — Taxonomy

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the domain model used throughout Career Copilot.

Rather than describing database schemas or implementation details, it defines how Career Copilot understands the career world through structured domain objects.

These objects become the shared language between Product, AI Reasoning, and Engineering.

---

# 2. Taxonomy Philosophy

Career Copilot does not reason over free text.

It reasons over structured domain objects.

Every reasoning process transforms structured objects into new structured objects while preserving authenticity and traceability.

The taxonomy is implementation-independent.

Its purpose is to define **what exists in the career domain**, not **how it is stored**.

---

# 3. Domain Layers

Career Copilot organizes all objects into three layers.

```text
Evidence
        │
        ▼
Reasoning
        │
        ▼
Expression
```

---

## 3.1 Evidence Layer

Evidence represents factual information provided by the candidate.

These objects are immutable.

Examples:

- Candidate
- Experience
- Achievement
- Project
- Education
- Certification

---

## 3.2 Reasoning Layer

Reasoning objects are inferred by Career Copilot.

They represent the system's understanding of professional capability.

Examples:

- Capability
- Skill
- Transferable Skill
- Opportunity
- Gap
- Confidence
- Evidence Link

---

## 3.3 Expression Layer

Expression objects are generated for communication.

They translate authentic capability into professional language.

Examples:

- Narrative
- Resume Bullet
- Tailored Resume
- Interview Package
- Cover Letter
- LinkedIn Summary

---

# 4. Core Objects

## Candidate

Represents the professional using Career Copilot.

---

## Experience

Represents a factual professional experience.

---

## Achievement

Represents a measurable accomplishment within an experience.

---

## Project

Represents a significant piece of work demonstrating professional capability.

---

## Education

Represents formal educational background.

---

## Certification

Represents officially recognized qualifications.

---

## Evidence

Represents factual information supporting reasoning.

Evidence is never generated by AI.

---

## Skill

Represents a specific professional skill demonstrated through evidence.

---

## Capability

Represents a broader professional ability inferred from one or more pieces of evidence.

Capabilities are reasoning objects rather than user inputs.

---

## Transferable Skill

Represents a capability applicable across industries or job functions.

---

## Opportunity

Represents the target role or career opportunity.

---

## Gap

Represents the difference between current capability and target opportunity.

---

## Confidence

Represents the system's confidence in an inferred conclusion.

---

## Narrative

Represents professionally optimized language generated from authentic evidence.

---

## Resume Bullet

Represents an individual resume statement.

---

## Tailored Resume

Represents the final resume optimized for a specific opportunity.

---

## Interview Package

Represents structured interview preparation generated from previous reasoning.

# 6. Object Lifecycle

All information within Career Copilot follows the same lifecycle.

```text
Evidence
        │
        ▼
Inference
        │
        ▼
Expression
```

The system never skips stages.

Professional expression must always originate from evidence through reasoning.

---

# 7. Design Rules

The taxonomy follows several mandatory rules.

## Evidence is Immutable

Evidence represents factual information and must never be modified by AI.

---

## Reasoning Requires Evidence

Every inferred capability must reference supporting evidence.

---

## Expression Requires Reasoning

Generated language must originate from inferred capabilities rather than directly from raw evidence.

---

## Complete Traceability

Every output should be explainable through the reasoning chain.

---

## Object Independence

Each object has a single responsibility.

Objects should not duplicate the responsibilities of other objects.

---

# 8. Future Evolution

The taxonomy is designed for long-term expansion.

Future domain objects may include:

- Career Goal
- Learning Plan
- Portfolio
- Networking
- Career Strategy
- Salary Expectation

New objects should extend the taxonomy rather than redefine existing objects.

---

# 9. Key Takeaway

Career Copilot understands careers through structured domain objects rather than free text.

This taxonomy provides the foundation for reasoning, explainability, scalability, and future product expansion.
